The thesis

Glitch art is when the failure of a medium becomes the artwork. That's the whole claim, and the rest of this site is seventeen stops of evidence. Rosa Menkman defines a technological glitch as a short-lived break in the expected flow of a digital system — and glitch art as the aesthetic, conceptual or metaphorical use of such errors. Hold onto both halves. The break is the thing that happens to the machine; the art is what an artist does with it.

So glitch is not "cool broken pixels." It is a way of showing the hidden structure of a medium. A clean image is a lie of omission: it pretends the system carrying it is invisible, weightless, neutral. A glitched image tears that pretence open and makes the apparatus visible — the codec, the screen, the compression, the interface, the protocol, the file format, the platform, the algorithm, and the ideology baked into all of them. The failure is a window. You look through the wreckage and see the machinery that was always there.

Failure as material

Most artists choose a medium and then try to behave well inside it — to keep the paint on the canvas, the signal clean, the file intact. Glitch artists treat the medium's limits as the point. Noise, damage, dropped frames, corrupted headers, exhausted memory, the moment a decoder gives up: that is the paint. You don't fight the failure mode, you compose with it.

This is why the technique matters more than the look. Anyone can stick a "broken" overlay on a poster. But the work that lasts tends to know something true about the actual structure it's wrecking — what a JPEG header expects, how motion vectors carry between frames, why an electron beam bends near a magnet. Below: not a filter. A real image, encoded to JPEG, its bytes corrupted, handed to the browser to decode the wreckage. The whole site is this gesture, repeated across a century.

A family tree, not a timeline

The history of glitch art is not one clean line. It's a family tree with a lot of ancestors who never met. Damaged film leads to electronic image manipulation, which feeds video art, which runs alongside computer art, which collides with glitch music, which spills into net.art, which hardens into databending and datamoshing, which gets absorbed by post-internet practice — and then handed, finally, to the new machines: the AI and platform glitches of right now.

damaged film → electronic image → video art → computer art → glitch music → net.art → databending → datamoshing → post-internet → AI / platform glitch.

None of these eras "replaced" the last. They keep running in parallel. A magnet on a CRT and a melting diffusion-model hand are doing the same conceptual work sixty years apart: making the machine confess. The pages that follow walk that tree branch by branch.

Three kinds of glitch

Before anything else, learn to tell these three apart. They can all be valid — but they carry different politics, and conflating them is how glitch gets reduced to a stock preset.

01
Accidental

The machine fails by itself. A corrupted file, a broken signal, a crashed screen. Nobody asked for it; the artist is a witness who decides to keep the result.

02
Induced

The artist forces the system to fail. A magnet held to a CRT, databending, datamoshing, circuit bending — deliberate violence aimed at a specific layer of the medium.

03
Simulated

The artist imitates glitch visually. An After Effects overlay, a Photoshop filter, a stock "cyberpunk glitch" preset. The look of failure without the failure.

The strongest glitch art usually knows something about the actual structure of its medium. Accidental glitch knows nothing and gets lucky. Simulated glitch knows only the surface. Induced glitch — the dangerous middle one — has to understand the system well enough to break it on purpose, and that understanding is what gives the work its teeth.

Media archaeology by force

Here is the line to land: glitch is media archaeology by force. Archaeologists dig slowly and respectfully through layers to recover what a system was. Glitch artists do the same dig, but with a hammer — they break the running machine and read the cross-section that falls out. The error is the excavation.
