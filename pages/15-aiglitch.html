Synthetic Failure

2020s

Old glitch: the file breaks. AI glitch: meaning breaks. We have walked from the magnet on the tube to the corrupted byte to the bleeding keyframe. Now the machine generates the image itself — and when it fails, it does not crash. It dreams.

A new kind of broken

Every glitch in this lecture so far has been a wound in a known object. You could point at it. The JPEG had a bad byte. The signal lost its sync. The codec predicted against the wrong past. The damage lived in the file, the wire, or the frame, and once you understood the format you understood the failure.

AI image and video systems do not fail like that. A diffusion model does not corrupt a picture — it never had one to corrupt. It hallucinates a plausible image out of noise, and when it loses the thread the result is not static or smear. It is wrong in a way that is fully rendered: a hand with six confident fingers, text that melts into letterforms that almost spell, an object that cannot physically exist photographed in perfect studio light. Anatomy folds. Identity drifts shot to shot. The model invents interfaces, buttons, signage, and reflections that refer to nothing. The picture is sharp, lit, color-graded — and meaningless.

This is the shift to hold onto. The earlier eras broke the carrier. This era breaks the content of the thought. The surface is clean; the meaning is the casualty.

The taxonomy, finally complete

It is worth stating the whole lineage in one breath, because the four eras of glitch are four different things that get sloppily called by one word:

  • OLD glitch — the file breaks. Bytes, headers, codecs. Databending, pixel sorting, the corrupted JPEG.
  • VIDEO glitch — the signal breaks. Sync, scan, chroma. Paik's magnet, the Vasulkas, dropout and tear.
  • NET glitch — the interface and the network break. Broken browsers, 404s, net.art, the page that is wrong on purpose.
  • AI glitch — meaning breaks. The model renders a confident lie.

And here is the part that makes it new rather than just another texture: the AI glitch is not only visual. It is epistemological. It breaks trust — you can no longer assume a photograph happened. It breaks authorship — who made this, the prompter or the dataset or the dead artists scraped into the weights? It breaks the body, the anatomy that every prior medium took as ground truth. It breaks memory and source, dissolving culture into a dreamlike compression where everything is vaguely cited and nothing is actually from anywhere. The glitch used to damage an image. This one damages your grounds for believing it.

The texture of mediated life

The reason this era matters is that the synthetic artifact is no longer an art-school exercise. It is the ambient surface of everything you look at. Platform compression is everywhere — TikTok, Instagram, YouTube, streaming, cloud render — re-encoding every clip down a pipeline of lossy passes until the image is a memory of a memory. Stack that on the machine-made image: surveillance cameras, machine vision, drone footage, LIDAR sweeps, photogrammetry reconstructions, GANs and diffusion models. Every one of them produces its own signature failure.

Capture that breaks into the uncanny

Photogrammetry is the honest example, because the seam is visible. You feed a system dozens of photographs and ask it to triangulate a solid world. Where capture is clean, you get a mesh. Where capture is thin — a shiny surface, a moving edge, a shadowed gap — the algorithm guesses, and you get holes, stretched skin, and melted geometry: an uncanny mesh that looks like an object remembered by something that was not paying attention. The glitch here is structural, baked into the model, and it sits right on the line between accurate and hallucinated.

Artists want the failure

This is not only a problem to be solved; it is material. A 2023 arXiv paper on artistic control over the glitch in AI-generated motion capture makes the point directly: artists working with these systems routinely hit unexpected glitches, and rather than suppress them they want to explore those failures as creative substance — to steer the breakage, not just clean it up. That is the whole posture of this lecture, arrived at again from the other side. The machine's mistake is the medium.
