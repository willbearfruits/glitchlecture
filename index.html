The core idea

Glitch art = when the failure of a medium becomes the artwork. Rosa Menkman defines a technological glitch as a short-lived break in the expected flow of a digital system, and glitch art as the aesthetic, conceptual or metaphorical use of such errors.

A clean image pretends the system is invisible. A glitched image exposes the system: the codec, the screen, the compression, the interface, the protocol, the file format, the platform, the algorithm, the ideology. The history isn't a single line — it's a family tree with many ancestors:

damaged film → electronic image → video art → computer art → glitch music → net.art → databending → datamoshing → post-internet → AI / platform glitch.

KIND 01
Accidental

The machine fails by itself. A corrupted file, a broken signal, a crashed screen.

KIND 02
Induced

The artist forces the system to fail. Magnet on a CRT, databending, datamoshing, circuit bending.

KIND 03
Simulated

The artist imitates glitch visually. A filter, an overlay, a "cyberpunk glitch" preset. Less dangerous — unless it's conceptually sharp.

The strongest glitch art usually knows something about the structure of its medium. A datamosh knows codecs. A CRT bend knows electron beams. A JODI page knows HTML.
